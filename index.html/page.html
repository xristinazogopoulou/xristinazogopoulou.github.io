Falling Carefully

Chong Wai uses mannequins in his exhibition to refer to John Locke’s idea of involuntary falling.

“The man does not voluntarily fall, nor could the man avoid falling. The unintentional action of falling is no longer a self-autonomous movement dominated by our body and will.”

Chong in his work questions the inescapable consequences of falling by subverting the predicted collapse.
